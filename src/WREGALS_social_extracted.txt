WREGALSBroadcast System PRDVersion 1.0 – Auction-Centric Creator Broadcast Layer1. Executive OverviewThe WREGALS Broadcast System is a controlled, identity-driven communication layer embedded within the auction platform.It enables verified public figures (celebrities, athletes, influencers) to publish short-form posts to:Announce upcoming auctionsShare collectible storiesShowcase itemsBuild anticipationStrengthen fan engagementThe system is strictly one-directional:Only verified personalities can create postsGeneral users can view, like, reshare, and share externallyNo public commenting (Phase 1)The Broadcast feature must enhance auction participation without transforming WREGALS into a generic social media platform.2. Strategic ObjectivesThe Broadcast system must:Increase auction anticipation and bidding intensityImprove user retention between auctionsStrengthen creator supply lock-inMaintain premium brand positioningAvoid social media chaos and noiseDrive measurable uplift in GMV3. User Roles & Permissions3.1 Verified CreatorsOnly verified public figures can:Create postsUpload media (image/video)Link posts to auctionsEdit or delete their postsView engagement analyticsVerification required before broadcast privileges are granted.3.2 General UsersGeneral users can:View postsFollow creatorsLike postsReshare postsShare posts externallyReceive notificationsGeneral users cannot:Create postsComment (Phase 1)Reply to postsQuote-post4. Post Structure & FormatEach broadcast post includes:Creator profile imageVerified badgeCreator nameTimestampText (max 300 characters)Optional image or videoOptional linked auction cardLike countReshare countShare buttonNo threaded replies.No comment box.No nested discussions.Posts must be chronological (not algorithmic).5. Content Scope & Atmosphere GuidelinesBroadcast content should align with the WREGALS identity:Upcoming auction previewsCollectible storytellingItem legacy narrativesBehind-the-scenes preparationDrop countdown messagesSold archive highlightsPersonal milestone artifactsThe platform maintains the right to remove content that:Is politicalIs religiousIs inflammatoryViolates brand positioningDamages auction credibilityAtmosphere must remain premium and auction-centric.6. Interaction Design6.1 LikeSingle-tap engagementVisible countNo dislike functionality6.2 ReshareReshare adds post to user’s profile activity sectionNo added commentary allowed (Phase 1)No quote resharing6.3 External ShareUsers can share to:WhatsAppInstagramTwitterLinkedInCopy linkExternal shares link directly to auction page or creator profile.7. Creator Profile ArchitectureEach creator profile contains:Header with verified badgeBio sectionCareer highlightsUpcoming Auctions sectionPast Auction ArchiveBroadcast feed (chronological)Follower countMedia galleryProfiles must maintain clean, editorial aesthetic.8. Homepage IntegrationHomepage must remain auction-first.Recommended layout:Live AuctionsUpcoming DropsFeatured CreatorsRecent Broadcast PostsBroadcast feed must not dominate homepage.Auction listings remain primary focus.9. Notification SystemUsers who follow a creator receive:New post notificationUpcoming drop reminderAuction live notificationPush notifications must be controlled to avoid spam.Creators cannot manually spam notifications without platform limits.10. Analytics (Creator Side)Creators should have access to:Post impressionsLike countReshare countClick-through rate to auctionFollower growthAuction conversion from postAnalytics dashboard may become monetizable in future premium tiers.11. Moderation & GovernanceEven without comments, governance is required.Platform must:Approve verified creatorsModerate post contentAllow reporting of postsRemove content violating policyMaintain audit logsGrievance redressal system must be active.12. Performance RequirementsThe Broadcast feed must:Load within 2 secondsSupport high-resolution mediaHandle peak auction trafficPrevent UI clutterAvoid infinite-scroll addiction mechanicsThe goal is professional clarity, not dopamine loops.13. Risk ConsiderationsPotential risks include:Platform identity dilutionContent controversyFeed overshadowing auctionsCreator spam behaviorNotification fatigueTo mitigate:Strict posting guidelinesEditorial oversightAuction-first homepageNotification throttling14. Success MetricsBroadcast system success will be measured by:Increase in auction participation rateIncrease in average final bid valueIncrease in repeat bidder rateFollower growth per creatorGMV uplift per broadcast-linked auctionTarget:At least 20% increase in auction engagement within 12 months of launch.15. Strategic Positioning StatementThe WREGALS Broadcast System is not social media.It is a controlled, identity-driven communication layer designed to amplify auctions and deepen creator–collector relationships.It must enhance transaction value without compromising premium positioning.
