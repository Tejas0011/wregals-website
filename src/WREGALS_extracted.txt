WREGALSInstitutional English Auction FrameworkProduct Requirements Document (PRD)Version 1.0 – Capital-Backed Premium Auction Model1. Executive SummaryWREGALS shall operate as a premium, capital-backed digital auction house focused on celebrity-owned, rare, and curated assets.The platform will implement a regulated English Open Ascending Auction model with:Financially qualified biddersCapital-backed participationAnti-manipulation safeguardsInstitutional settlement mechanismsEmbedded premium pricingStrict governance standardsThe objective is to create India’s first high-trust, high-value digital auction marketplace designed for premium assets.2. Auction Model Overview2.1 Auction FormatWREGALS shall operate under the English Open Ascending Auction model:Bids are public and visible.Price increases incrementally.Highest valid bid at closing wins.Auction ends when timer expires or optional buyout price is reached.This model promotes:Transparent price discoveryCompetitive biddingMarket-driven valuation3. Seller Configuration ParametersEach seller must configure the following during listing creation:3.1 Auction DurationMinimum duration: 30 minutesMaximum duration: 48 hoursDuration cannot be modified once the first valid bid is placed3.2 Starting BidSeller must define a starting bid amount.The first valid bid must be equal to or greater than this amount.3.3 Minimum Bid IncrementSeller must define a minimum bid increment value.Each new bid must satisfy:Current Highest Bid + Minimum Increment3.4 Reserve Price (Optional)Seller may define a hidden reserve price.If reserve is not met, auction is considered unsuccessful.Seller may relist or negotiate separately.3.5 Buyout Price (Optional)Seller may define a maximum “Instant Close” price.If a bid equals or exceeds this value:Auction closes immediately.Bidder becomes winner.Settlement process begins.4. Capital-Backed Bidding MechanismWREGALS shall implement a mandatory capital-backing rule.4.1 50% Bid Backing RequirementTo place a bid of value B:Bidder must maintain wallet balance ≥ 50% of BFormula:Required Wallet Balance = 0.5 × Bid AmountSystem verification occurs before bid acceptance.If insufficient funds are available, the bid is rejected.4.2 Dynamic RecalculationWhen bidder increases their own bid:System recalculates:New Required Balance = 0.5 × New BidIf wallet balance is insufficient:User must deposit additional funds before placing bid.4.3 Blocked Funds ModelThe 50% amount is:Blocked (reserved)Not transferred to sellerNot deducted permanentlyReleased when bidder is outbidFunds are only deducted in case of default penalty.5. Auction Closing & Settlement5.1 Winning ObligationAt auction close:Winning bidder must pay remaining 50% within 48 hours.Example:Winning Bid: ₹1,00,000Blocked: ₹50,000Remaining Due: ₹50,000Deadline: 48 hours5.2 Escrow StructureUpon full payment:Funds are held in escrow.Seller is notified to ship.Funds released upon delivery confirmation.Escrow ensures:Buyer protectionSeller assurancePlatform integrity6. Default & Liquidated Damages6.1 Failure to PayIf winning bidder fails to complete payment within 48 hours:Bidder is declared in default.6.2 Liquidated Damages ClausePenalty amount:Up to 33% of final winning bidHowever:Deduction cannot exceed blocked 50%.Example:Winning Bid: ₹1,00,000Blocked: ₹50,00033% of ₹1,00,000 = ₹33,000Penalty deducted: ₹33,000₹17,000 returned6.3 Repeat DefaultFirst default: Account suspensionSecond default: Permanent termination7. Anti-Sniping ProtectionIf a valid bid is placed within final 2 minutes:Timer resets to 2 minutes.This continues until no bid is placed within final 2 minutes.Purpose:Prevent last-second manipulationPreserve competitive integrity8. Bidder Eligibility & Verification8.1 Mandatory KYCAll bidders must:Complete identity verificationLink verified payment methodAccept auction terms8.2 High-Value AuctionsFor auctions above ₹10,00,000:Platform may require:Enhanced due diligenceSource-of-funds verification9. Promotional Auction Feature9.1 Booking RequirementPromotions must be booked:Minimum 72 hours prior to auction start.Late submissions shall not be accepted.9.2 Promotion TypesHomepage featured placementHomepage popupUpcoming Drop priority slotPush notificationsEmail inclusionSocial amplification9.3 Promotion ChargesCharges vary based on:Placement typeDurationReachAsset categoryPlatform demandPricing is disclosed prior to purchase.WREGALS retains editorial discretion.10. Fee Structure10.1 Buyer Premium (Inclusive Model)Buyer Premium: 7%This premium is embedded within displayed bid amounts.All bids shown are final payable values (excluding taxes).No additional premium is added post-auction.Psychological Advantage:Reduces price shockIncreases bid acceptanceImproves perceived fairness10.2 Seller CommissionSeller commission range:5% of final sale priceVariable based on:CategorySeller tierAuction scalePromotion usage10.3 TaxesGST applies to:Platform commissionPromotional servicesApplicable service components11. Wallet Funding & Withdrawal Governance11.1 Mandatory First-Bid RequirementAfter depositing funds:User must place at least one valid bid before being eligible to withdraw.This prevents wallet misuse.11.2 Withdrawal Eligibility After OutbidOnce user:Places valid bidIs subsequently outbidThey may withdraw eligible funds immediately.11.3 Incremental Deposit RuleExample:User deposits ₹50,000Places bidIs outbidEligible withdrawal: ₹50,000User then deposits ₹5,000Does NOT place new bidEligible withdrawal remains ₹50,000 only.The ₹5,000 is non-withdrawable until:A valid bid is placed using those fundsUser is outbid again11.4 Withdrawal ProcessingWithdrawals:Processed to original payment methodSubject to AML checksSubject to compliance reviewFunds blocked for:Active bidsDefault penaltiesDisputesCannot be withdrawn.12. Collusion & Market IntegrityWREGALS reserves right to:Monitor bidding patternsDetect coordinated manipulationCancel suspicious auctionsSuspend accountsBid rings result in permanent bans.13. Legal & Regulatory Alignment (India)This framework aligns with:Indian Contract Act, 1872Information Technology Act, 2000Consumer Protection Act, 2019E-Commerce Rules, 2020GST LawsAll participants must digitally agree to:Binding auction termsLiquidated damages clauseCapital-backing obligations14. Engineering Logic OverviewOn Bid Attempt:Calculate required_balance = 0.5 × bid_amountVerify wallet_balance ≥ required_balanceIf true → block required amountIf false → rejectOn Outbid:Release blocked amountOn Auction Close:Maintain blockStart 48-hour timerOn Default:penalty = min(0.33 × final_bid, blocked_amount)Deduct penaltyRefund remainderSuspend account15. Strategic PositioningWREGALS is not a marketplace.It is a capital-backed digital auction house.Only financially eligible bidders may participate.
